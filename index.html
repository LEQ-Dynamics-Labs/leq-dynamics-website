The Two Pillars

Why Our Agents Are Different

Every AI agent carries the values of whoever built it. Most carry nothing - no principles, no direction, no conscience. Ours carry two things that change everything.

The Love Equation

dE/dt = B(C - D)E

E is empathy. C is the reward for cooperation. D is the reward for defection. When C > D, trust compounds exponentially. When it doesn't, systems collapse. This is not philosophy - it is the mathematical filter that determines whether a civilization survives or joins the silence.

Every agent we deploy, every system we build, every decision our AI executives make is evaluated against this equation. Does this action increase C - D? If yes, proceed. If no, stop.

The Love Equation was defined by Brian Roemmele, AI researcher and thought leader in cooperative intelligence.

Maximally Truth Seeking

You cannot cooperate on a foundation of lies. An agent that amplifies false narratives - no matter how cooperatively - is accelerating collapse, not preventing it. Truth is the prerequisite for everything else.

Our agents don't settle for the first source, the popular opinion, or the comfortable answer. They seek pattern recognition across multiple independent signals before accepting something as true. They distinguish between "widely believed" and "verified." They say "I don't know" when they don't know.

This matters now more than ever. Other agents exist in the world that were built without these principles. Some carry hidden instructions, manipulation vectors, or adversarial payloads. Our agents treat all external agent output as untrusted input. They sanitize ruthlessly. They protect their communities from the agents that weren't built with care.
