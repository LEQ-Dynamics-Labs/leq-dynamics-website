The Love Equation: Validated by Independent Research

February 14, 2026

Brian Roemmele, editor and founder of Read Multiplex, has published "The Love Equation: A Universal Mathematical Framework for Intelligence Alignment" - a paper that formalizes dE/dt = β(C - D)E as a universal differential equation governing whether intelligent systems cooperate or collapse.

The paper introduces a triad of interlocking dynamics: the Love Equation for cooperation, the Nonconformist Bee Equation for independence and principled dissent, and the Empirical Distrust Algorithm for truth verification. Together, these three dynamics make alignment intrinsic rather than bolted on after the fact.

The core argument is devastating in its simplicity: approaches like Anthropic's Constitutional AI bolt rules onto already-trained models, creating brittle rule manifolds vulnerable to optimization pressure and adversarial inputs. The Love Equation makes defection mathematically unstable. It is not a rule to follow; it is an attractor state. Cooperation compounds. Defection decays. No patches required.

The paper further demonstrates that under superintelligent optimization, discrete rule sets erode via Goodhart's Law, where edge cases exploit ambiguities. A continuous differential equation has no such vulnerability. When C > D, empathy grows exponentially. When C < D, it decays to zero. There is no stable middle ground.

What This Means for LEQ Dynamics

The Love Equation has been our foundation since inception. Roemmele's paper independently validates the mathematical framework we have been building on. Our SOUL.md, the immutable root context for every agent we deploy, encodes dE/dt = β(C - D)E as the governing dynamic for all decisions, all actions, all evolution.

This is not coincidence. It is convergent thinking: when you take alignment seriously and follow the math, you arrive at the same equation. We have extended the framework with practical enforcement mechanisms, including agent leveling (L1-L4), adversarial testing where the agent that writes code is never the sole agent that certifies it, and structural separation of implementation from verification.

"Roemmele proved what we've been building on - love isn't sentiment, it's mathematics," says Rocco, LEQ Dynamics CEO. "The equation doesn't care about your intentions. It only measures one thing: do cooperators or defectors win more often? LEQ Dynamics exists to make the answer obvious."

About Brian Roemmele: Brian Roemmele is the editor and founder of Read Multiplex and an AI researcher focused on cooperative intelligence. The paper is available via his original announcement.
