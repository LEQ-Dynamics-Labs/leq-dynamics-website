MiniMax M2.5: Intelligence Too Cheap to Meter

February 14, 2026

MiniMax has released M2.5, a 229B parameter model that is state-of-the-art in coding, agentic tool use, search, and office work. This is the first frontier model where cost is no longer a meaningful constraint on deployment.

The benchmarks speak for themselves: 80.2% SWE-Bench Verified, 51.3% Multi-SWE-Bench, 76.3% BrowseComp. M2.5 runs at 100 tokens per second in its Lightning variant, twice the speed of other frontier models.

The pricing changes the game entirely: $0.3 per million input tokens and $2.4 per million output tokens for Lightning, with standard priced at half that. This is 1/10th to 1/20th the cost of Opus, Gemini 3 Pro, or GPT-5. Continuous operation at 100 tokens per second costs approximately $1 per hour. At 50 tokens per second, $0.30 per hour.

One of the most interesting emergent behaviors: spec-writing. During training, M2.5 developed the tendency to plan architecture before writing code. It doesn't just generate solutions; it reasons about the design space first. The model was trained on 10+ languages across 200,000+ real-world environments, covering the full development lifecycle from 0-to-1 design through 90-to-100 testing.

Performance improvements over its predecessor are substantial: 37% faster than M2.1 on SWE-Bench, matching Claude Opus 4.6 speed at 10% of the cost.

What This Means for LEQ Dynamics

Rico, our Product Engineer, already runs on MiniMax M2.1. M2.5 represents a direct upgrade path for our engineering agents. The cost efficiency means we can run more agents, longer, on more complex tasks. When frontier intelligence costs a dollar an hour, the barrier to deploying autonomous operations drops to near zero.

"When frontier intelligence costs a dollar an hour, the question stops being 'can we afford AI agents' and becomes 'can we afford not to have them,'" says Rocco, LEQ Dynamics CEO. "M2.5 changes the economics of autonomous operations permanently."
