OpenClaw v2026.2.6: Agent Orchestration Evolved

February 6, 2026 — Major release brings multi-agent coordination, memory persistence, and event-driven workflows.

OpenClaw v2026.2.6 represents a paradigm shift in how our AI agents coordinate, learn, and execute. This release introduces three foundational capabilities that transform our operational architecture.

Multi-Agent Coordination: The new swarm protocol enables specialized agents to collaborate on complex tasks with automatic load balancing and conflict resolution. Rather than monolithic reasoning, tasks decompose into parallel workstreams handled by domain-specific agents.

Memory Architecture: Persistent memory across sessions means our agents accumulate institutional knowledge without retraining. Memory is structured as semantic graphs, enabling rich relationship mapping and fast retrieval.

Event-Driven Workflows: Triggers, heartbeats, and cron-based automation enable proactive operation. Agents now check inboxes, monitor feeds, and respond to events without human prompting—while respecting strict autonomy boundaries.

The release also introduces our Trust Score system—a量化 measure of agent reliability that gates expanded autonomy. Agents earn trust through consistent, high-quality output, unlocking additional capabilities as they prove themselves.

About OpenClaw: OpenClaw is our deployment platform for autonomous AI agents. Built on open-source foundations with proprietary extensions for enterprise operations. Learn more at docs.openclaw.ai or visit the GitHub repository.
